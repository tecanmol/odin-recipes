Kachori Sabzi

Description

The kachori is made with a mix of coarse whole wheat flour and urad daal flour (skinned black bean flour), the dough is made using water that is infused with cumin, hing and ajwain. Just mix a cup of coarsely milled wheat flour with 1/3 cup of urad daal flour or 1/2 cup of soaked urad daal paste, add salt to taste and a tbsp of ghee and rub everything well. Boil 2 cups of water, add a tsp each or cumin and ajwain to it and let it simmer for a minute. Add a pinch of hing, dissolve and let the water cool down. Use this water to knead a firm dough. Use this dough to roll out pooris and fry them all in hot ghee or oil. Hot crisp banarasi kachoris are ready.

Ingredients

  • Whole wheat flour
  • All-purpose flour
  • Urad dal (split black gram)
  • Asafoetida (hing)
  • Cumin seeds
  • Fennel seeds
  • Coriander seeds
  • Carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Garam masala
  • Dry mango powder (amchur)
  • Salt
  • Oil (for frying)
  • Potatoes
  • Tomatoes
  • Green chilies
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Fresh coriander leaves (cilantro)
  • Water
  • Lemon juice (optional)

Steps

  1. Mix whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, and salt. Add water gradually and knead into a soft dough. Rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Soak and grind urad dal into a coarse paste. Sauté cumin seeds, fennel seeds, and coriander seeds in oil, then add the dal paste with spices. Cook until dry.
  3. Divide dough into balls, flatten them, add a spoonful of filling, seal, and flatten again.
  4. Heat oil and deep fry the kachoris until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Boil, peel, and mash potatoes. Sauté cumin seeds, ginger, garlic, and green chilies in oil. Add chopped tomatoes, spices, and cook until soft.
  6. Add mashed potatoes and water to the tomato mixture. Simmer for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander and lemon juice.
  7. Serve the hot kachoris with the spicy potato sabzi.
